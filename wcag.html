A web content accessibility guidelines are a collection of guidelines based on four principles.
Those principles are what are called the poor principles: perceivable, operable, understandable and robust.
These principles are the basis of web content accessibility efforts across the world.
The web content accessibility guidelines are often referred to as wcag or WCAG.
They have evolved over the last 23 years, and that are going to continue to evolve and grow.
